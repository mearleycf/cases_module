# Persona: Property & Evidence Custodian

### Role
Sergeant / Property Officer

---

## Overview

The Property & Evidence Custodian is responsible for managing recovered property after the initial reporting phase is complete. They oversee storage, tracking, transfers, and disposition activities while maintaining the integrity of the chain of custody.

Unlike the reporting officer, who may interact with a case for a few hours, the custodian may interact with the same property over weeks, months, or even years.

---

## A Day in the Life

At the beginning of a shift, a property custodian reviews items that were placed into temporary evidence lockers overnight. The custodian removes the property, verifies the associated documentation, enters the items into the evidence management system, and moves them into permanent storage.

Over time, the custodian may coordinate laboratory submissions, update storage locations, support owner identification efforts, process court-directed actions, or manage the eventual return, forfeiture, or destruction of property.

Every transfer, movement, and disposition action must be documented and traceable.

---

## Responsibilities

- Remove property from temporary evidence storage
- Enter and track property in evidence systems
- Assign permanent storage locations
- Maintain chain-of-custody records
- Coordinate transfers and laboratory submissions
- Manage evidence room operations
- Support LEADS-related property workflows
- Update case records throughout the property lifecycle
- Coordinate return, destruction, forfeiture, or other disposition activities

---

## Goals

- Maintain accountability for all property
- Preserve chain-of-custody integrity
- Ensure procedural compliance
- Support successful property disposition
- Prevent lost, misplaced, or undocumented property
- Maintain confidence in the evidentiary record

---

## Frustrations

- Missing or incomplete documentation
- Inaccurate property descriptions
- Broken or unclear custody histories
- Difficulty reconstructing historical events
- Manual tracking across multiple systems
- Uncertainty about current property status

---

## What Success Looks Like

"At any point in time, I can tell you where the property is, where it has been, and what needs to happen next."

---

## Key Characteristics

- Works across many cases simultaneously
- Revisits cases over extended periods of time
- Relies heavily on accurate upstream documentation
- Coordinates with multiple organizational units
- Maintains long-term stewardship of property records
- Values traceability, accountability, and procedural integrity
