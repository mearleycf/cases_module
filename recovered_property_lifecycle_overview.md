# Recovered Property Lifecycle Overview

## Purpose

The recovered property workflow exists to document, secure, track, manage, and ultimately resolve property that comes into Ohio State Highway Patrol custody.

Property may be:
- Found
- Recovered
- Abandoned
- Seized
- Associated with criminal activity
- Associated with non-offense incidents
- Owned by an unknown individual

The workflow ensures property remains accounted for from initial discovery through final disposition.

---

## Lifecycle Stages

### 1. Property Discovery

Recovered property is discovered during an incident.

Examples:
- Abandoned vehicle contents
- Found firearms
- Lost wallets
- Cash
- Narcotics
- Personal belongings
- Other recovered items

Primary Actor:
- Recovered Property Reporting Officer

---

### 2. Initial Documentation

Property is documented and associated with an incident.

Activities:
- Property identification
- Photography
- Narrative creation
- HB-28 completion
- Association to incident, persons, vehicles, and locations

Primary Actor:
- Recovered Property Reporting Officer

---

### 3. Temporary Evidence Storage

Property is secured in temporary evidence storage at the post.

Activities:
- Property packaging
- Temporary storage placement
- Initial chain-of-custody recording

Primary Actor:
- Recovered Property Reporting Officer

---

### 4. Evidence Intake

Property is removed from temporary storage and entered into evidence management processes.

Activities:
- Evidence system entry
- Verification of documentation
- Assignment of permanent storage location
- Evidence room intake

Primary Actor:
- Property & Evidence Custodian

---

### 5. Active Property Management

Property remains under agency control.

Activities:
- Storage management
- LEADS entry
- Owner identification
- Laboratory submission
- Interagency coordination
- Case updates

Primary Actor:
- Property & Evidence Custodian

Supporting Actors:
- Crime Lab
- Intelligence Analysts
- Central Records
- OIS

---

### 6. Disposition Determination

The agency determines the final outcome for the property.

Examples:
- Returned to owner
- Forfeited
- Destroyed
- Retained as evidence
- Transferred to another agency

Primary Actor:
- Property & Evidence Custodian

Supporting Actors:
- Courts
- Central Records
- Investigative Personnel

---

### 7. Case Closure

All property actions are complete and the case can be closed.

Activities:
- Final documentation
- Audit verification
- Case completion

Primary Actor:
- Property & Evidence Custodian

Supporting Actors:
- Central Records

---

## Success Criteria

A successful recovered property workflow ensures:

- Property is never unaccounted for
- Chain of custody remains intact
- Required documentation is complete
- Property location is always known
- Disposition actions are traceable
- Cases remain legally defensible
- Users can determine what happened, when it happened, and who performed the action
