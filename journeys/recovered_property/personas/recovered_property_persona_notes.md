What I would simplify

The generated image still has too much “UX persona template” DNA in it.

I’d remove:

- Tools & Systems Used
- At a Glance
- Who / Where / When
- Works With

Not because they’re wrong.

Because they dilute the important information.

⸻

What I’d replace it with

I’d devote that space to:

Key Decisions

Examples:

Recovered Property Reporting Officer

- Is this recovered property?
- What should be documented?
- What needs to be photographed?
- What belongs on the HB-28?
- Is the property secure?

For the custodian:

- Where should property be stored?
- Does ownership need to be researched?
- Is transfer required?
- Is disposition ready?
- Can the case be closed?

Those decisions are what drive the workflow.

⸻

For an OSHP audience

Honestly, if you put two persona cards side-by-side:

Recovered Property Reporting Officer

and

Property & Evidence Custodian

with a shared lifecycle diagram underneath, I think the troopers and sergeants would immediately recognize themselves in it.

That’s the test I’d use.

If a post commander walks up and says:

“Yep, that’s basically what happens.”

then the persona is doing its job.

If they walk up and start discussing whether the person is 35 or 45 years old, then the persona has become decorative instead of useful.

For this project, I think workflow-centered personas are much stronger than traditional demographic personas. The image should support that story, not compete with it.
