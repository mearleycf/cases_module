# Persona: Property & Evidence Custodian

## Role
Sergeant / Property Officer

---

## Overview

The Property & Evidence Custodian is responsible for managing recovered property after the initial reporting phase is complete. In the found wallet scenario, they retrieve the wallet from temporary storage, verify the reporting officer's documentation, continue the custody record, and manage the work needed to identify, notify, return, or otherwise dispose of the property.

Unlike the reporting officer, who may interact with the wallet for a short period of time, the custodian may interact with the same property over weeks, months, or even years.

---

## A Day in the Life

At the beginning of a shift, a property custodian reviews a recovered property case for a wallet turned in by a citizen at a turnpike rest area. The custodian removes the wallet from the temporary evidence locker, verifies the HB-28, narrative, wallet description, cash amount, driver's license, credit cards, and locker information, then enters or updates the property in the evidence management system.

The custodian may support owner identification, determine whether LEADS or another system is needed, coordinate owner notification, move the wallet into permanent storage, and manage return or disposition if the wallet is not claimed.

Every transfer, movement, owner contact, return, and disposition action must be documented and traceable.

---

## Responsibilities

- Remove property from temporary evidence storage
- Verify HB-28, narrative, property description, and locker information
- Confirm the wallet contents are documented clearly enough for continued handling
- Enter and track property in evidence systems
- Assign permanent storage locations
- Maintain chain-of-custody records
- Support owner identification and notification workflows
- Manage evidence room operations
- Support LEADS-related property workflows
- Update case records throughout the property lifecycle
- Coordinate return, destruction, forfeiture, or other disposition activities

---

## Goals

- Maintain accountability for all property
- Preserve chain-of-custody integrity
- Ensure procedural compliance
- Identify and return property to the rightful owner when possible
- Support successful property disposition
- Prevent lost, misplaced, or undocumented property
- Maintain confidence in the evidentiary record

---

## Frustrations

- Missing or incomplete documentation
- Inaccurate property descriptions
- Missing finder, location, or date/time information
- Unclear wallet content itemization
- Unclear ownership or notification status
- Broken or unclear custody histories
- Difficulty reconstructing historical events
- Manual tracking across multiple systems
- Uncertainty about current property status

---

## What Success Looks Like

"At any point in time, I can tell you where the property is, where it has been, and what needs to happen next."

---

## Key Characteristics

- Works across many cases simultaneously
- Revisits cases over extended periods of time
- Relies heavily on accurate upstream documentation
- Coordinates with multiple organizational units
- Maintains long-term stewardship of property records
- Values traceability, accountability, and procedural integrity
- Needs clear handoff information from the Reporting Officer
