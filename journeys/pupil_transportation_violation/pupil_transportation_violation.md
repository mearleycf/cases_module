Pupil Transportation Violation — Non‑Offense Workflow

Purpose

This document outlines how to handle Pupil Transportation Violation cases in OTIS 2.0. These incidents involve violations during school bus operations or issues discovered during inspections. Accurate records support administrative action and ensure student safety.

Examples of Violations

- Use of Electronic Devices: A school bus driver uses a mobile phone or other electronic device while operating the bus.
- Inspection Failures: A bus inspection uncovers mechanical defects (e.g. brakes, lights) that violate safety standards.
- Student Left on Bus: A student is inadvertently left on the bus after routes are completed.
- Railroad Crossing Violations: Driver fails to stop or properly clear tracks during railroad crossing.
- Unapproved Boarding: Driver allows unauthorized individuals to board the bus or allows students to board/exit at non‑approved stops.
- Fights or Disorder: Physical altercations occur on the bus requiring intervention.

Required Data Elements

- Bus Operator: Name and contact information of the driver.
- School or District: Name of the school or school district responsible for the bus.
- Violation Type: A drop‑down list should allow selection of the specific violation (electronic device use, inspection failure, etc.).
- Location & Time: Where and when the violation occurred.
- Passengers & Witnesses: Number of students on board and any witnesses.
- Inspection Report / Evidence: Upload inspection reports, photographs, or other documentation.
- Corrective Action: Fields to record citations issued, administrative actions, or re‑inspection dates.

Workflow

1. Incident Creation: Create a non‑offense case with nature code Pupil Transportation Violation (914).
2. Person & Organization Entry: Add the bus driver and assign roles (Operator, Witness, etc.). Add the school or bus company as an organization entry.
3. Violation Details: Select the violation type and enter location, time, and conditions. Attach supporting documents or photos.
4. Narrative: Describe what occurred, including how the violation was discovered, actions taken on scene, and any immediate corrective measures.
5. Corrective Action: Record any citations issued to the driver, warnings given, or referrals to the school district. Schedule a re‑inspection if required.
6. Case Closure: After corrective actions are taken and any administrative processes are complete, close the case and retain documentation for recordkeeping.

Notes

- Clear documentation ensures transparency and accountability in pupil transportation safety.
- OTIS should support reporting on trends in bus violations to assist schools and regulators in improving safety measures.
