Cash / Currency Seizure — Non‑Offense Workflow

Purpose

This document outlines how Cash / Currency Seizure cases should be recorded in OTIS 2.0. Cash seizures often occur during joint task force operations or highway interdictions. Accurate tracking ensures equitable sharing and compliance with forfeiture laws.

Key Points

- Task Force Operations: Cash seizures frequently occur as part of multi‑agency task forces (e.g. HIDTA – High Intensity Drug Trafficking Area). Multiple agencies may be involved in the same seizure.
- Equitable Sharing Agreements: Funds must be divided among participating agencies according to equitable sharing agreements. Accurate seizure documentation is critical for fair distribution.
- Forfeiture vs. Evidence: Cash can be seized either as evidence in a criminal case or for civil forfeiture. OTIS must differentiate between these purposes and track the status of the currency accordingly.

Workflow Steps

1. Incident Creation: Create a case using nature code Cash / Currency Seizure (919). Include other nature codes if additional activities occurred (e.g. assist other agency).
2. Agency Listing: Add all participating agencies to the case with their percentage or share of the seizure (if known). Record contact information for each agency.
3. Property Entry: Use the property tab to enter the amount of cash seized. Document denominations, packaging (e.g. bundles, rubber bands), and any unique identifiers (e.g. serial number ranges if recorded).
4. Disposition Category: Choose Evidence if the money will be held pending criminal charges, or Forfeiture if the money is to be forfeited. This determines the next steps and the eventual distribution of funds.
5. Chain of Custody: Document who seized the currency, who counted it, and who has custody. Provide receipts or signed property control forms.
6. Equitable Sharing Tracking: Record the equitable sharing agreement reference number (if applicable). When funds are disbursed, update the case with the distribution breakdown and attach documentation.
7. Narrative: Describe the circumstances of the seizure, the legal basis (e.g. probable cause for forfeiture), and interactions with task force partners.
8. Case Closure: After the forfeiture process or court proceedings conclude, document the final disposition. Ensure funds were distributed per agreements.

Notes

- Multi‑agency involvement makes accurate documentation essential to prevent disputes.
- Transparency: OTIS should provide a report showing initial seizure amount, forfeited amount, evidence amount, and distribution details.
