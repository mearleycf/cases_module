Assist Other Agency (AoA) — Standard Workflow

Purpose

This document defines how Assist Other Agency cases should be captured in OTIS 2.0. These cases occur when the Ohio State Patrol supports another law enforcement agency but does not have primary jurisdiction. Examples include officer‑involved shootings handled by a local police department, pursuits initiated by a sheriff’s office, or task force operations where OSP provides manpower or equipment.

Characteristics

- Standard Data: AoA cases generally involve the same data elements as other cases—persons, property, locations, vehicles, narratives, and attachments. The main difference is that OSP is assisting another agency.
- Property Tracking: If OSP takes custody of property on behalf of another agency (e.g. seized cash during a task force warrant), it must be tracked in OTIS. Standard property and evidence workflows apply.
- Multi‑Role Persons: Individuals may have complex roles (e.g. suspect in the other agency’s investigation but victim in a separate complaint). Use the multi‑role person model to reflect these situations.
- Examples:
  - Officer‑involved shooting where OSP did not discharge a weapon but assisted with scene control.
  - Pursuit initiated by the sheriff’s office where OSP assisted and handled traffic control.
  - Columbus task force search warrant resulting in currency seizure; OSP supported entry and evidence collection.
  - Vehicle theft unit task forces (e.g. Franklin County) using OSP resources.

Recommended Workflow

1. Incident Creation: Create an incident with nature code Assistance to Other Law Enforcement Agencies (921). Include other nature codes as needed (e.g. cash currency seizure, computer crimes).
2. Agency Information: Enter the name and contact information of the primary agency. Include a primary agency contact person.
3. Persons & Roles: Add persons involved. Assign roles using the multi‑role drop‑down (e.g. Suspect, Victim, Witness, OSP Officer, Other Agency Officer).
4. Property & Evidence: If property is collected, follow standard property intake workflow. Indicate whether property will be returned to the primary agency or handled by OSP.
5. Narrative: Describe the nature of the assistance, the actions OSP personnel took, and any property or persons handled. Note the extent of OSP’s involvement and when responsibility transferred back to the primary agency.
6. Disposition: When assistance concludes, close the case after confirming all property and persons have been transferred or released appropriately.

Notes

- AoA cases should not replace dedicated case types (e.g. Computer Crimes). Use AoA for situations where OSP’s role is limited to assisting another agency.
- Provide clear distinction between OSP‑controlled evidence and evidence under the primary agency’s control to avoid chain‑of‑custody confusion.
