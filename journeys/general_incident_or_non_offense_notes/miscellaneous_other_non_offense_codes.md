Miscellaneous Non‑Offense Codes — Additional Notes

This addendum summarizes other non‑offense nature codes and observations gathered from recent discovery sessions. These codes are used infrequently or have not yet been fully developed. They are included here for completeness and future consideration.

Traffic‑Related Violence (593)

- Individuals may simultaneously be both a victim and a suspect. The proposed multi‑role person model accommodates this.
- There are no unique OTIS requirements beyond standard person/property/narrative fields.

Lost/Stolen Division Evidence (793)

- Involves OSP evidence that has gone missing, historically linked to mailing drugs through the USPS.
- Very few cases have been filed since 2020. Treat as a Lost Property Report (see Lost Property workflow) with additional fields to indicate division evidence.

Recovered Drugs from State Property

- Occurs when narcotics or paraphernalia are found on state property (e.g. rest areas, state buildings) without a known suspect.
- These may fit under miscellaneous cases; handle as recovered property or recovered drugs depending on whether charges are filed.

Assist Other Agency Variants

- Bait Vehicle Deployment (923): Primarily used by vehicle theft units. Could be treated as an Assist Other Agency case or have a dedicated nature code. Currently, details are usually recorded in the narrative.
- Institutional K‑9 Sweeps (824): Last used in 2022. There are fields on the incident screen, but usage is rare. Consider retiring or hiding this code unless needed again.

Motor Vehicle Fire (710) & Other Fires (720)

- Used infrequently—most fire incidents are captured in crash reports.
- Unless the fire is unrelated to a crash, these codes may be obsolete. Review with stakeholders to determine if they should remain available.

Background Checks (950–959)

- These codes (Cadet Candidates, Investigations, etc.) are legacy. Background checks are now handled outside OTIS. These codes may be deprecated.

Property Availability

- For any of these rarely used codes, ensure that property and person tabs remain available (see Property on Non‑Offense Workflows and Person Roles Update documents).

⸻

While many of these codes are obsolete or seldom used, they should be documented so that future development can either re‑implement or retire them. Regularly reviewing utilization statistics will help determine whether to keep, modify, or remove these nature codes.
